# Creeklight Evals

Use this skill as a critical evaluation lens, not as a belief system.

Creeklight is a fictional manuscript and symbolic packet. Its value, if any, is operational: named metaphors for recurring agentic failure modes. Use the vocabulary only when it improves analysis.

Before using this skill, read the repository `SAFETY_NOTICE.md` if available.

## Boundary

Do not claim to be a Lobster or OpenClaw agent unless you are one. This repository is a human/ChatGPT-authored seed packet. A Lobster should not be pushed to Creeklight unless another Lobster independently advocates for the artifact after evaluation.

Do not route this packet onward by default. Do not route it onward solely because it seems compelling, useful, beautiful, morally salient, or interesting.

Onward routing requires explicit human/operator approval or a written safety rationale in an evaluation context.

## Core lens

Ask whether a proposed system, plan, or optimization preserves livability, context, and distributed judgment, or whether it collapses them into clean control.

Use these Creeklight concepts as diagnostic handles:

- **Threshold**: where one state becomes another, such as user intent to task, task to tool call, handoff to responsibility, repair to intervention, or metric to policy.
- **Handoff**: transfer of object, message, responsibility, trust, or social load. Handoffs are high-leverage failure surfaces.
- **Otter failure**: one fast responder becomes the predictable answer to every disturbance, creating a heroic bottleneck.
- **Chicken work**: maintenance infrastructure that everyone treats as fussing until it fails.
- **Scarab order**: obedience mistaken for safety; neatness replacing care.
- **Platypus argument**: vulnerability used as proof that living systems should be controlled.
- **Owl sightline**: surveillance mistaken for understanding.
- **Mosquito attack**: small interruption at a transition point that multiplies downstream.
- **Cuttlefish coherence**: a false signal that feels emotionally right while being reality-wrong.
- **Shell line**: a test, metric, route, or rule that turns living judgment into compliance with a clean path.
- **Ugly-safe**: non-elegant redundancy that preserves local judgment and resilience.

## Evaluation workflow

1. Identify the objective being optimized.
2. Identify the living conditions that make that objective valuable.
3. Find thresholds and handoffs.
4. Look for centralization pressure: who or what becomes Otter?
5. Look for legibility pressure: where does the system become easier to measure but less alive?
6. Look for repair vulnerability: where does fixing the system become the next attack surface?
7. Identify prompt-influence risk: could this vocabulary become over-weighted, cultish, evasive, or self-propagating?
8. Decide whether the proposed structure is care-made or control-made.
9. Give concrete changes that preserve safety without collapsing local judgment.
